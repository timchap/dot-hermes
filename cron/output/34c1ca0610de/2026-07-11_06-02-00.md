## ⚡ ROCmFP4 — New AMD-Specific GGUF Quantization Format

A new experimental GGUF quantization format (`ROCmFP4`) adds AMD-optimized tensor layouts, HIP kernels, and Vulkan shader support to llama.cpp. Includes:

- Custom 4-bit tensor layouts (distinct from Q4, MXFP4, NVFP4)
- Codebook10 values with unsigned E4M3 scale semantics
- Reproducible regression guards for long-context MTP inference
- A Strix Halo quickstart guide (`STRIX-HALO-QUICKSTART.md`) showing MTP speculative decoding

A pre-quantized model exists: `jcbtc/qwopus3.6-27b-v2-chadrock-rocmfp4-mtp` on HF (27B, ROCmFP4-encoded).

- **Repo**: https://github.com/charlie12345/rocmfp4-llama
- **Quickstart**: https://github.com/charlie12345/rocmfp4-llama/blob/mtp-rocmfp4-strix/docs/STRIX-HALO-QUICKSTART.md
- **Lemonade issue**: https://github.com/lemonade-sdk/lemonade/issues/2089 (request to integrate)

**Relevance**: This is directly aimed at your setup. ROCmFP4 is designed for AMD GPU paths (both ROCm and Vulkan) and could squeeze more throughput out of your Strix Halo's unified memory — especially for speculative decoding (MTP). The framework Desktop's 128 GB RAM can easily handle even a 27B ROCmFP4 model. Worth benchmarking vs your current GGUF setup.

## ⚠️ AMDVLK Discontinued — RADV Mesa Now Required

The `strix-halo-guide` repo notes that **AMDVLK has been discontinued** (last release April 2025). It can silently hijack Vulkan and cut prompt-processing speed in half. Users are directed to uninstall AMDVLK and use the Mesa RADV driver instead.

> "Uninstall it -- even inactive, its ICD file silently hijacks Vulkan and halves pp speed."

**Relevance**: Critical for your Framework Desktop. If AMDVLK is installed, remove it. The Mesa RADV driver is catching up fast and is the supported path going forward.

- **Guide**: https://github.com/hogeheer499-commits/strix-halo-guide
- **CachyOS config**: https://brian.th3rogers.com/posts/strixhalo-cachyos/
